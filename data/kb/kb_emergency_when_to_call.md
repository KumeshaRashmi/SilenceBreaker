# When to Seek Immediate Help (General Information)

Source note: General safety information. NOT a substitute for emergency services.

Some situations need urgent help. Consider contacting your local emergency number or a crisis service immediately if:
- You or someone else is in immediate physical danger.
- Someone has been seriously hurt or needs medical attention.
- There are threats to life, or weapons are involved.
- You feel you cannot keep yourself or your children safe right now.

If it is not safe to speak, some emergency services in certain countries allow silent or alternative ways to signal that you need help; check what is available where you live.

If you are having thoughts of harming yourself, please reach out to a crisis line or a trusted person right away. You deserve support and you do not have to face this alone.

After the immediate danger has passed, a recognised support organisation can help with next steps such as safety planning and ongoing support.
