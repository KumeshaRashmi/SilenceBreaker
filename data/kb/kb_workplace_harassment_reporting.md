# Reporting Workplace Harassment (General Information)

Source note: General guidance. NOT legal advice.

If you experience harassment at work, these general steps may help:
1. Keep a private record of incidents: dates, times, what was said or done, and any witnesses.
2. Review your organisation's harassment, grievance, or code-of-conduct policy. It is often in the staff handbook or HR portal.
3. Where you feel able, report to a trusted manager, HR, or a designated reporting officer in writing, so there is a record.
4. Keep copies of relevant communications and store them somewhere private (ideally not only on a work device).
5. Note any anti-retaliation protections your employer or local law provides; retaliation for reporting in good faith is often prohibited.

You can also seek external support, such as a relevant labour authority, a union or staff representative, or an advice service.

Reporting can feel daunting, and it is reasonable to seek advice before deciding how to proceed. You are entitled to a workplace free from harassment.

This information is general and may not reflect the laws or policies in your country or workplace. For your specific situation, contact a qualified advisor, a relevant labour authority, or a support organisation in your area.
