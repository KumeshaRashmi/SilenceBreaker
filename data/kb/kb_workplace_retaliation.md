# Workplace Retaliation: Your Rights and Protections

## What is workplace retaliation?

Retaliation happens when an employer punishes you for:
- Reporting harassment or abuse
- Refusing unwanted advances or requests
- Filing a formal complaint
- Speaking to HR
- Taking sick leave due to harassment

Common retaliation tactics:
- Reducing hours or shifts
- Denying promotions
- Negative performance reviews
- Reassignment to worse positions
- Exclusion from meetings or opportunities
- Threats or intimidation
- Termination (in some cases)

## Your legal rights

**In most countries, retaliation is ILLEGAL:**
- You have the right to report without fear of punishment
- Your employer cannot legally retaliate
- You are protected if you acted in good faith
- Retaliation itself is grounds for complaint

**Documentation is key:**
- Keep records of what you reported and when
- Note the retaliation actions that followed (with dates)
- Save emails and messages showing the connection
- Witness statements help
- Your employer must show a legitimate, separate reason for actions

## Steps to take

**Immediate actions:**
1. **Write it down** – document the retaliation with dates and details
2. **Save evidence** – emails, messages, performance reviews, schedules
3. **Report formally** – file a written complaint with HR (keep a copy)
4. **Tell someone** – inform a trusted coworker or manager
5. **Keep safe** – don't discuss with people who may report back to harasser

**Formal complaints:**
- File with your HR department
- Request a written response
- Follow your company's grievance procedure
- Keep copies of everything you submit
- Request a meeting to discuss

**External help:**
- Contact your country's labor board or employment tribunal
- Consult an employment lawyer (many offer free consultations)
- File with your equal opportunity/civil rights commission
- Document everything for legal proceedings

## Workplace rights overview

You have the right to:
- A safe workplace free from harassment
- Report concerns without fear
- Work the hours promised to you
- Fair treatment regardless of gender, race, or other protected status
- Reasonable accommodations if needed
- Protection from retaliation
