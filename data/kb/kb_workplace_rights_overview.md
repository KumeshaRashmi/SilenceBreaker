# Workplace Rights: General Overview (General Information)

Source note: High-level, general information. NOT legal advice; rights vary by country and contract.

Many workplaces and legal systems recognise that employees are entitled to:
- A working environment free from harassment, bullying, and discrimination.
- A clear process for raising concerns or grievances.
- Protection from retaliation for reporting concerns in good faith.
- Confidential handling of complaints, as far as reasonably possible.

Employers often have duties to prevent and address harassment, to investigate complaints fairly, and to take appropriate action.

The exact rights, definitions, time limits, and procedures depend heavily on your country, sector, and employment contract. What counts as harassment and how it must be handled is defined by local law and policy.

If you are unsure about your rights, consider speaking with:
- Your HR department or a staff/union representative.
- A relevant government labour or equality authority.
- A qualified employment advisor or advice service.
