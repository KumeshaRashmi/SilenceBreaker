# Legal Options and Aid (General Information)

Source note: General awareness information. NOT legal advice. Laws vary by country and situation.

Abuse survivors may have legal options available to them, even if the situation does not involve physical violence. Understanding general options can help you make informed decisions with the help of a qualified professional.

## Common legal options people explore
- **Protective orders / injunctions**: A court order that can legally prohibit someone from contacting or approaching you. Known as a restraining order (US), non-molestation order (UK), or apprehended violence order (Australia).
- **Criminal reporting**: Domestic abuse, harassment, coercive control, and stalking are criminal offences in many countries. Reporting to police is a personal decision; support workers can help you think this through.
- **Housing rights**: In some jurisdictions, a victim of domestic abuse may have rights to remain in a shared home or access emergency housing.
- **Civil remedies**: Depending on the country, civil courts can award damages or other remedies in some cases of harassment or abuse.

## Getting legal help
- **Legal aid**: Many countries provide free or subsidised legal advice for people fleeing abuse. Eligibility criteria vary.
- **Pro bono services**: Some law firms and charities offer free legal advice to survivors.
- **Support organisations**: Specialist domestic abuse services often have trained legal advocates or can refer you to one.
