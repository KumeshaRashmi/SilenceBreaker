# Keeping a Record (General Information)

Source note: General practical information. NOT legal advice.

Keeping a clear, factual record can be helpful if you later choose to report a situation or seek support. General tips people find useful:
- Note the date, time, and place of each incident.
- Write down what was said or done, as factually as possible, soon after it happens.
- Record the names of anyone present who saw or heard what happened.
- Keep copies of relevant messages, emails, or other communications.
- Store your records somewhere private and secure, ideally not only on a device that another person can access.

If you are worried a device or account is being monitored, consider keeping records on a separate, safer device or with a trusted person.

A record does not commit you to any action. It simply preserves information so that you have options later.

How records are used in any formal process depends on local rules and the type of process. For advice specific to your situation, contact a qualified advisor or a recognised support organisation.
