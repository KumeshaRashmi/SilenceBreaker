# Privacy and Staying Safer Online (General Information)

Source note: General digital-safety information. NOT professional security advice.

If you are looking for support, your privacy matters. General digital-safety ideas people find useful:
- If you think a device or account may be monitored, consider using a safer device (for example, a public library computer or a trusted friend's phone).
- Be aware of browser history and consider clearing it, or using private browsing, where it is safe to do so.
- Check whether location sharing is switched on in apps and devices.
- Be cautious about shared accounts, shared cloud storage, and saved passwords.
- Trust your instincts: if something does not feel safe, take your time.

Changing settings can sometimes alert another person, so consider whether any change might increase risk in your specific situation.

This prototype does not store your conversation or personal data. It is designed to provide general information without requiring you to identify yourself.
