# Safety Planning Basics (General Information)

Source note: General safety-awareness information. NOT professional or emergency advice.

A safety plan is a personal, practical set of ideas to help someone stay safer, whether they choose to stay, leave, or are unsure. Plans are individual; what is safe for one person may not be safe for another.

General ideas people find useful:
- Identify safer spaces and people you trust who you could contact.
- Keep important documents, some money, keys, and essential items somewhere accessible if it is safe to do so.
- Think in advance about how you could reach help quickly, and a calm way to explain you need to leave a situation.
- Be aware that shared devices or accounts may be monitored; consider using a safer device when looking for help.
- Plan for children, pets, or dependents if relevant.

If you are considering leaving, the period around leaving can be a higher-risk time, so talking it through with a trained support worker is strongly encouraged.

Only you know your situation best. A recognised local support organisation can help you build a plan tailored to your circumstances, confidentially and without pressure.

This is general information only. In an emergency, contact your local emergency services. For tailored help, contact a qualified support service in your area.
