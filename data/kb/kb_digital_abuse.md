# Digital Abuse and Online Control

## What is digital abuse?

Digital abuse (also called cyber abuse or online abuse) includes:
- Monitoring your location using GPS or phone tracking
- Reading your messages and emails
- Checking your social media and passwords
- Installing spyware or tracking apps
- Controlling your online access
- Sharing private information online without permission
- Threatening to share intimate images
- Impersonating you online
- Harassing you through messages
- Isolating you from online support groups

## Common tracking and monitoring methods

**Location tracking:**
- Find My Device / Find My iPhone / Google Location Sharing
- Spyware apps (installed without your knowledge)
- AirTags or GPS trackers in your bag
- Fake family tracking apps
- Phone bill tracking

**Message monitoring:**
- Screenshots of your chats
- Reading your emails without password
- Social media access
- Dating app monitoring
- Bank account access

**Digital isolation:**
- Deleting your social media accounts
- Preventing internet access
- Blocking your phone
- Preventing contact with friends online
- Controlling gaming or entertainment access

## Recognizing digital abuse

**Warning signs:**
- Your abuser knows details you didn't tell them
- Your passwords keep changing
- Your phone acts strangely (heating up, battery drains fast)
- Unfamiliar apps are installed
- Your location history shows checks
- Messages go missing
- Your social media has unfamiliar activity
- Your abuser accuses you of things you didn't do

## Safety steps

**If you suspect tracking or monitoring:**

1. **Check your phone's location settings**
   - Settings → Privacy → Location Services
   - Check what apps have location access
   - Disable for suspicious apps
   - Turn off location completely when safe

2. **Check for unfamiliar apps**
   - Look for hidden app folders
   - Check recently downloaded apps
   - Delete anything you don't recognize
   - Be careful of apps with innocent names (fake maps, calculators)

3. **Secure your passwords**
   - Change all passwords (from a safe device/location)
   - Use strong passwords (12+ characters, mix of letters/numbers)
   - Turn on two-factor authentication
   - Don't save passwords in browsers
   - Use a password manager

4. **Create a safe communication method**
   - Use a phone/email only your abuser doesn't know about
   - Use a public library or work computer for important communications
   - Use messaging apps that delete messages (Signal, Telegram)
   - Create coded language with trusted people
   - Delete app and browser history regularly

5. **Protect your social media**
   - Change passwords and security questions
   - Check connected apps (remove ones you don't recognize)
   - Review tagged photos and remove
   - Set privacy to private/friends only
   - Deactivate if not safe to use

6. **Check accounts for unauthorized access**
   - Email: Review login activity and remove devices
   - Banking: Check recent transactions and linked accounts
   - Cloud: Check connected devices and remove unknowns
   - Social media: Review app permissions and logins

## Getting help safely

**Tell someone you trust:**
- A friend, family member, or counselor
- Your boss or HR (workplace option)
- A domestic abuse helpline (trained to help)
- Police (if threats or serious monitoring)

**Keep evidence:**
- Take screenshots of monitoring activities
- Save threatening messages
- Document dates and times of incidents
- Keep location history reports
- Take photos of tracking devices

**Get a new phone if possible:**
- Use cash to buy a burner phone
- Don't give the number to your abuser
- Keep it somewhere safe

## Protecting yourself long-term

**Technical safety:**
- Use strong, unique passwords
- Enable automatic security updates
- Use antivirus software
- Keep apps updated
- Don't use public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities
- Use a VPN if safe to do so

**Behavioral safety:**
- Tell trusted people where you're going
- Vary your routine (don't go same places at same times)
- Be aware if you're followed
- Tell your workplace you're in an unsafe situation
- Ask for your schedule to be kept private

**If leaving the relationship:**
- Change all passwords before contact ceases
- Delete photos and messages
- Wipe all devices clean
- Create new email accounts
- Disable cloud sync temporarily
- Get a new phone number
- Update emergency contacts
