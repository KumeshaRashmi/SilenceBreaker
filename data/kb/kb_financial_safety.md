# Financial Safety and Economic Abuse (General Information)

Source note: General awareness information. NOT financial or legal advice.

Financial or economic abuse is a form of coercive control in which one person controls another's access to money, employment, or financial resources. It can make it very difficult for a person to leave an abusive situation.

## Signs of financial abuse
- Being given an "allowance" and having to account for every purchase.
- Having bank accounts or cards controlled or monitored by a partner.
- Being prevented from working, studying, or having your own income.
- Having debt run up in your name without your knowledge or consent.
- Having access to your own money restricted or denied.

## Steps people find helpful

**Immediate financial safety**
- If possible, open a personal bank account at a different bank from any shared account, using a safe address (e.g. a trusted friend, family member, or support organisation).
- Keep small amounts of emergency cash in a safe place if you can.
- Make a note of important financial information (account numbers, benefits entitlements) and store it safely.

**Longer-term steps**
- Check your credit report for unknown accounts or debts in your name.
- Contact your bank — many banks have specialist teams for customers experiencing financial abuse.
- Seek advice from a financial counsellor, benefits advisor, or debt charity.
