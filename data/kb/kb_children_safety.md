# Protecting Children in Abuse Situations (General Information)

Source note: General awareness information. NOT legal or professional advice.

When children are present in a household where abuse occurs, their safety is an important additional consideration. Children can be affected by witnessing abuse even if they are not directly harmed.

## Signs children may be affected
- Changes in behaviour, sleep, or school performance.
- Becoming withdrawn, anxious, or unusually aggressive.
- Expressing fear of going home or being around certain adults.
- Physical signs of harm or neglect.

## General steps people find helpful
- Keep a private record of any concerns about the children.
- Where safe to do so, include children in your safety plan — identify where they can go and who they can call.
- Teach children age-appropriate safety information, such as a trusted adult they can speak to.
- If you believe a child is in immediate danger, contact emergency services or a child protection authority.

## Reporting concerns about a child
In most countries, anyone can report a concern about a child's safety to a statutory child protection service. You do not need to be certain that abuse is occurring — sharing a genuine concern is enough. Reports can often be made anonymously.

## Verified child protection contacts

**United Kingdom**
- NSPCC Helpline: 0808 800 5000 (free, 24/7) | help@nspcc.org.uk | https://www.nspcc.org.uk
- Childline (for children): 0800 1111 (free, 24/7) | https://www.childline.org.uk
- Local authority children's services (search by area on gov.uk)

**United States**
- Childhelp National Child Abuse Hotline: 1-800-422-4453 (24/7) | https://www.childhelp.org
- Child Welfare Information Gateway: https://www.childwelfare.gov (state reporting numbers)

**Australia**
- Child Protection Helpline (NSW): 132 111 | https://www.facs.nsw.gov.au
- Kids Helpline: 1800 551 800 | https://kidshelpline.com.au

**Sri Lanka**
- National Child Protection Authority (NCPA): 1929 (free, 24/7) | https://www.ncpa.gov.lk
- Department of Probation and Child Care Services: +94 11 2186234

Sources: NSPCC (nspcc.org.uk), Childhelp (childhelp.org), NCPA Sri Lanka (ncpa.gov.lk). Verified June 2025.

This document is general information only. If a child is in immediate danger, contact emergency services immediately.
