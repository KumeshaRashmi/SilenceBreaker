# Looking After Yourself (General Information)

Source note: General wellbeing information. NOT a substitute for mental-health care.

Experiencing abuse or harassment can be exhausting and frightening, and it is normal to feel a wide range of emotions, including fear, anger, sadness, numbness, or confusion. None of this is your fault.

General things that some people find supportive:
- Reaching out to someone you trust, so you are not carrying it alone.
- Contacting a confidential support line where you can talk things through.
- Allowing yourself rest and small routines that feel steadying.
- Being patient and kind with yourself; there is no "right" way to feel.

If you are struggling with your mental health, or having thoughts of harming yourself, please reach out to a crisis service or a health professional. Support is available and you deserve it.

Talking to a trained support worker or counsellor can help you process what is happening and think through your options at your own pace.

This document offers general wellbeing information only and is not a substitute for professional care. In an emergency, contact local emergency services.
