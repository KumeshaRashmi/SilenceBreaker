# Recognising Abuse (General Information)

Source note: General awareness information. NOT legal, medical, or professional advice.

Abuse can take many forms and is not always physical. Common patterns people describe include:
- Physical harm: hitting, pushing, grabbing, or any unwanted physical force.
- Emotional or psychological harm: constant criticism, humiliation, intimidation, or threats.
- Controlling behaviour: restricting where someone can go, who they can see, or what they can do.
- Financial control: limiting access to money, bank accounts, or the ability to work.
- Sexual harm: any sexual activity without freely given consent.

Abuse is never the fault of the person experiencing it. It can happen in relationships, families, and workplaces, and to people of any gender, age, or background.

A situation may be abusive even if it does not involve physical violence. Feeling afraid, controlled, isolated, or constantly "on edge" around a particular person can be a sign that something is wrong.

If you recognise these patterns in your own situation, you are not alone and support is available. Consider reaching out to a trusted person or a recognised support organisation in your area who can talk through your specific circumstances confidentially.

This document is general information only and may not reflect the laws or services in your country. For advice specific to your situation, contact a qualified advisor or local support service.
